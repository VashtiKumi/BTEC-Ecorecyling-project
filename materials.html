Paper & Cardboard

Recyclable: Newspapers, magazines, cardboard boxes, office paper

Not Recyclable: Wax-coated paper, tissues, paper towels

Tip: Remove staples and tape before recycling

Recycling Rate: 68%

Plastic

Recyclable: Bottles, containers with recycling codes 1-7

Not Recyclable: Plastic bags, styrofoam, broken plastic

Tip: Clean containers and check recycling codes

Recycling Rate: 9%
üç∂

Glass

Recyclable: Bottles, jars (clear, brown, green)

Not Recyclable: Mirrors, light bulbs, ceramics

Tip: Remove lids and rinse containers

Recycling Rate: 33%
ü•´

Metal

Recyclable: Aluminum cans, steel cans, foil

Not Recyclable: Paint cans with residue, aerosol cans

Tip: Aluminum can be recycled infinitely

Recycling Rate: 75%
üì±

Electronics

Recyclable: Phones, computers, batteries, cables

Special Handling: Take to certified e-waste centers

Tip: Wipe personal data before recycling

Recycling Rate: 20%

Organic Waste

Compostable: Food scraps, yard waste, paper towels

Not Compostable: Meat, dairy, oils, pet waste

Tip: Start a home compost bin

Composting Rate: 5%

Recycling Tips & Best Practices

Clean Before Recycling

Rinse containers to remove food residue. Clean materials are more likely to be recycled successfully.

üîç

Check Local Guidelines

Recycling rules vary by location. Check with your local recycling center for specific guidelines.

üì¶

Break Down Boxes

Flatten cardboard boxes to save space and make transportation more efficient.

üö´

Avoid Contamination

Don't mix different materials. One contaminated item can ruin an entire batch.

Special Items

Batteries, electronics, and hazardous materials need special handling at designated centers.

Reduce First

Remember: Reduce, Reuse, then Recycle. Prevention is better than recycling.
