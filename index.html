About Recycling Materials

What is Recycling? Recycling is the process of collecting and transforming waste materials into reusable items to prevent waste, conserve resources, and protect the environment.

Why Recycle? Recycling reduces the need for extracting, refining, and processing raw materials which all pollute air and water. It also conserves natural resources, reduces greenhouse gases, and supports a circular economy.

How Recycling Works: Recyclable materials are collected, sorted, cleaned, and reprocessed into new products. This process varies by material and recycling facility, but the goal is always to extend the lifecycle of resources.

What Can Be Recycled? Common materials include paper, cardboard, plastics (PET, HDPE), glass, aluminum cans, and steel. Knowing your local recycling guidelines is key to effective recycling.
